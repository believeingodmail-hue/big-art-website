Dedicated Collection Page

BelieveinGOD Collection

A devotional body of work built as a sanctuary: quiet enough for prayer, refined enough for international collectors, and rooted enough to carry ancestral presence.

The BelieveinGOD Collection is the heart of B.I.G Art Collections. It treats belief not as ornament, but as architecture: a way of ordering space, restraint, color, material, and memory.

Each work is composed like a threshold. Dark fields hold stillness. Gold becomes revelation. Earthen surfaces recall Edo bronze, palace walls, ancestral shrines, and the dignity of inherited names.

Artist Biography

Izoduwa Precious

Izoduwa Precious is the visionary artist behind B.I.G Art Collections and the BelieveinGOD Collection. The practice moves between devotion, African contemporary abstraction, spiritual portraiture, and the material intelligence of earth, cloth, ash, metal, and light.

Precious approaches art as testimony: a disciplined act of remembering God, honoring ancestry, and creating objects that can live with emotional permanence in homes, galleries, museums, and contemplative interiors.

Edo Heritage / Ancestral Memory

A living archive of bronze, earth, prayer, and return.

01

Benin visual intelligence

Compositions draw from the disciplined grandeur of Edo visual culture: frontal power, ceremonial stillness, textured surfaces, and objects that feel entrusted with memory.

02

Ancestry as presence

The work does not illustrate ancestry; it creates space for it. Layered pigment, charcoal, and textile suggest names, prayers, and histories carried through generations.

03

Sacred contemporary language

Ancient memory is translated into a contemporary gallery vocabulary: vast shadow, restrained typography, ceremonial gold, and museum-grade calm.

Artist Statement

Artist statement

“I create from a place of reverence. Gold becomes light, darkness becomes stillness, and every figure is held between ancestry and prayer.”

The work is minimalist by intention: enough space for the spirit to breathe, enough texture for memory to remain present, and enough beauty for the viewer to slow down.
